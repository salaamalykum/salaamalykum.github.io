When I hear about underground mosques, I immediately think of a magazine I read as a kid. It talked about a country in Central Asia during the Soviet era where people dug a hole in the ground and propped it up with a few wooden beams to escape religious suppression.\n\n\n
\n\n\n
People would sneak inside to perform namaz. But this mosque in Singapore is different. The mosque owned property in the business district. After signing an agreement with United Overseas Bank (UOB), both sides decided the mosque would trade its above-ground property for part of the space on the first basement level of the UOB building to use as a mosque.\n\n\n
\n\n\n
